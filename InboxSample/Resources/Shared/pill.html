• Telmisartan

    One 40mg tablet

    Take with food

    About Telmisartan

    Telmisartan is an Angiotensin Receptor Blocker (ARB) that shows high affinity for the angiotensin II type 1 (AT1) receptors, has a long duration of action, and has the longest half-life of any ARB.

    • Used for the management of hypertension
    • Do not use during the last six months of pregnancy
    • You may experience dizziness, lightheadedness, blurred vision, or back pain as your body adjusts to the medication.

  • Omeprazole

    One 20mg tablet

    Take with water

    About Omeprazole

    Each packet contains either 2.8 mg or 11.2 mg of omeprazole magnesium (equivalent to 2.5 mg or 10 mg of omeprazole), in the form of enteric-coated granules.

    • Omeprazole is used to treat symptoms of gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) and other conditions caused by excess stomach acid.
    • Not for immediate relief of heartburn symptoms.
    • Heartburn is often confused with the first symptoms of a heart attack. Seek emergency medical attention if you have chest pain or heavy feeling, dizziness, pain spreading to the arm or shoulder, sweating, nausea or vomiting, and a general ill feeling.

  • Regletin

    Two 30mg tablets

    Take with water

    About Regletin

    Regletin is a non-selective beta-blocker used in the treatment of hypertension, edema, ventricular tachycardias, and atrial fibrillation.

    • One of the adrenergic beta-antagonists used as an antihypertensive, anti-anginal, and anti-arrhythmic agent.
    • Has been used as the hydrochloride in the treatment of cardiac arrhythmias
Some Important Information
